Algebra 2 Excellence Bible — Part 3: Trigonometry

Core Principle: Trigonometry is coordinate geometry on a circle.

Do not memorize the unit circle. Construct it.

Foundation F7 — From Triangle to Coordinate Plane

Formal Definition:
An angle in standard position has its vertex at (0,0) and initial side on the positive x-axis.

Tier 1 — Coordinate Definitions

For a point (x,y) on a circle with radius r:

sinθ = y/r

cosθ = x/r

tanθ = y/x

Tier 2 — Unit Circle Upgrade

If r = 1 (unit circle):

sinθ = y

cosθ = x

sin²θ + cos²θ = 1

This identity comes directly from x² + y² = 1.

Tier 3 — Reciprocal Functions

cscθ = 1/sinθ

secθ = 1/cosθ

cotθ = 1/tanθ

Undefined when denominator = 0.

Foundation F8 — Quadrants & Reference Angles

Tier 1 — Sign Logic

ASTC:

  • Q1: All positive
  • Q2: Sine positive
  • Q3: Tangent positive
  • Q4: Cosine positive

Tier 2 — Reference Angle (Formal)

The reference angle is the acute angle formed between the terminal side and the x-axis.

Example: 150° → Reference angle = 30°.

Foundation F9 — Radians (Arc Length Definition)

Tier 1 — Definition

θ (radians) = arc length / radius

π radians = 180°

2π radians = 360°

Tier 2 — Why Radians Matter

Radians eliminate unit distortion in calculus. They make trig functions behave naturally under limits.
