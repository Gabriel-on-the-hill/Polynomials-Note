Algebra 2 Excellence Bible — Part 7: Rational Functions (Refined)

Core principle: Division by zero is the electrical fence of mathematics. Do not touch it.

A rational function is just a fraction with an attitude (polynomials top and bottom).

F19 — The Domain (The Forbidden Zone)

Spiral link: Fractions (Algebra 1). You can never have 0 in the denominator.

Tier 1 — Set Denominator ≠ 0

The Rule: The domain is EVERYTHING except what makes the bottom zero.

Example: y = 1 / (x - 3)
Bottom is zero when x = 3.
Domain: {x | x ≠ 3} or (-∞, 3) U (3, ∞)

Tier 2 — Hole vs Asymptote

When you find a forbidden value (x=c), ask: Can I cancel it out?

  • Yes (Removable): It's a HOLE. The graph has a tiny gap there.
  • No (Non-removable): It's a VERTICAL ASYMPTOTE. The graph hits a wall and explodes to ∞.

Always factor top and bottom first.

F20 — Asymptotes (Walls & Horizons)

Spiral link: Polynomial Degrees (Part 2). It's a battle of strength between top and bottom.

F21 — Algebra with Rationals

Spiral link: Complex Fractions. Standard fraction rules apply.

Tier 1 — The Rules

  • Multiply: Straight across. (A/B)·(C/D) = AC/BD.
  • Divide: Keep-Change-Flip. (A/B)/(C/D) = (A/B)·(D/C).
  • Add/Sub: NEED Common Denominator. The "LCD" is the sewing kit that stitches them together.

Practice & Pitfalls

Tier 3 — Common Pitfalls

  • Canceling terms that are trapped in addition (e.g., canceling x in (x+1)/x). Forbidden.
  • Forgetting to check the domain of the simplified answer. The hole still exists even if you canceled it!
  • Solve: Cross-multiply ONLY when it's Fraction = Fraction. If it's Fraction + Fraction = Fraction, simplify first.

Worked Example — Graphing

Graph: y = (x+3)/(x-2)

  1. VA: Denom = 0 → x = 2.
  2. HA: Degrees equal (1 vs 1). Ratio of coeffs = 1/1 = 1. y = 1.
  3. Intercepts:
    • y-int (x=0): 3/-2 = -1.5.
    • x-int (y=0): Top = 0 → x = -3.
