3. Common Pitfalls & MCAP Traps Exam Prep

Trap #1: The "Freshman Dream"

Incorrect: \( (x + 3)^2 = x^2 + 9 \)

Correct: Box foil it! \( (x + 3)(x + 3) = x^2 + 6x + 9 \)

Trap #2: Canceling Terms in Fractions (Rationals)

Incorrect: \( \frac{x^2 - 4}{x^2 + 4} \) becomes \( \frac{-4}{4} = -1 \)

Correct: You cannot cancel terms! Only factors. \( x^2 - 4 \) is addition/subtraction. You must factor it to \( (x-2)(x+2) \) FIRST to see if any complete factors match.

Trap #3: Extraneous Solutions

When solving rational equations by multiplying by an LCD, check your final answers against the original denominators. If an answer makes an original denominator equal zero, it is mathematically illegal (Extraneous) and must be thrown out!

Trap #4: Forgetting the \(\pm\)

When solving \( x^2 = 16 \) by taking the square root of both sides, NEVER write just \( x = 4 \). It MUST be \( x = \pm 4 \).

4. Cumulative Mixed Practice

Instructions: Treat this like a mini-mock exam. Do not look at the solutions until you have attempted every problem.

  1. (Quadratics) Solve for \( x \) by completing the square: \( -2x^2 + 12x - 14 = 0 \)
  2. (Complex) Evaluate \( (3 - 5i)^2 \) and write in standard complex form.
  3. (Polynomials) Completely factor the cubic polynomial given that \( x = 2 \) is a known root: \( P(x) = x^3 - 7x^2 + 16x - 12 \)
  4. (Rationals) Add and simplify: \( \frac{x}{x-2} + \frac{4}{x+2} \)
  5. (Modeling) A rocket is launched. Its height \( h \) in feet after \( t \) seconds is given by \( h(t) = -16t^2 + 64t + 80 \). How many seconds until it hits the ground?
Step-by-step Solutions

1. Completing the Square:
Divide all by -2: \( x^2 - 6x + 7 = 0 \)
Move constant: \( x^2 - 6x = -7 \)
Half of -6 is -3, squared is 9: \( x^2 - 6x + 9 = -7 + 9 \)
Factor: \( (x - 3)^2 = 2 \)
Root: \( x - 3 = \pm\sqrt{2} \implies x = 3 \pm \sqrt{2} \)

2. Complex Exponent:
\( (3 - 5i)(3 - 5i) = 9 - 15i - 15i + 25i^2 \)
\( 9 - 30i + 25(-1) \)
\( 9 - 30i - 25 = -16 - 30i \)

3. Advanced Factoring:
Since \( x=2 \) is a root, divide \( P(x) \) by \( (x-2) \) using division.
Quotient is \( x^2 - 5x + 6 \).
Factor the quotient: \( (x-2)(x-3) \).
Original polynomial factored entirely: \( (x-2)(x-2)(x-3) \) or \( (x-2)^2(x-3) \).

4. Rational Addition:
LCD is \( (x-2)(x+2) \).
Top left: \( x(x+2) = x^2 + 2x \)
Top right: \( 4(x-2) = 4x - 8 \)
Combine over LCD: \( \frac{x^2 + 6x - 8}{x^2 - 4} \).

5. Rocket Modeling:
Hits ground when \( h(t) = 0 \).
\( -16t^2 + 64t + 80 = 0 \)
Divide out GCF (-16): \( t^2 - 4t - 5 = 0 \)
Factor: \( (t - 5)(t + 1) = 0 \)
Solutions: \( t = 5, t = -1 \). Reject the negative time.
Answer: 5 seconds.

5. MCAP Strategy Tips Testing Strategy

  • Read the Constraints: MCAP questions often trick you by placing domain restrictions secretly in the text like "where \( x > 0 \)".
  • Work Backwards on Factoring: If they ask for the factors of a polynomial and give a multiple-choice list, you don't actually have to factor it from scratch! Just distribute/FOIL the answer choices to see which matches the original problem.
  • Use Desmos to "Cheat" Legally: If an equation asks for roots, type the whole function into the built-in Desmos graphing calculator and visually tap the x-intercepts. You can verify your strict algebraic work instantly.
  • Check System Solutions Twice: When solving a linear-quadratic system, remember that picking the `x`-coordinate is only half the battle. You must plug it back in to find the `y`-coordinate. The MCAP will absolutely include the lone `x`-coordinate as a trap answer.
