Unit 2 — Part D: Rational Functions

Understanding the behavior of polynomials divided by polynomials.

1. Rational Expressions & Simplification MCAP Essential

A rational expression is a fraction where both the numerator and the denominator are polynomials. Since you cannot divide by zero, rational functions always have domain restrictions wherever the denominator equals zero.

Simplifying Rational Expressions

Rule #1 of Rationals: You cannot cancel terms that are added or subtracted. You can only cancel FACTORS.

  1. Factor the numerator completely.
  2. Factor the denominator completely.
  3. Cancel factors that appear in BOTH the numerator and the denominator.
  4. State domain restrictions based on the original, unfactored denominator.
Example: Simplify \( \frac{x^2 + 5x + 6}{x^2 - 4} \)

1. Factor top: \( (x + 2)(x + 3) \)

2. Factor bottom (difference of squares): \( (x + 2)(x - 2) \)

3. Domain restrictions: \( x \neq 2, x \neq -2 \) (because these make the bottom zero).

4. Cancel the common factor \( (x + 2) \).

Result: \( \frac{x + 3}{x - 2} \) for \( x \neq -2, 2 \).

🎯 Practice — Simplifying Rational Expressions

  1. (Simplify + Restrictions) Simplify \( \frac{x^2 - 9}{x^2 + x - 6} \) and state all domain restrictions.
  2. (Error Analysis) A student cancels: \( \frac{x + 3}{x^2 + 3} = \frac{1}{x} \). Explain why this is WRONG.
Answers

1. Factor: \( \frac{(x-3)(x+3)}{(x+3)(x-2)} = \frac{x-3}{x-2} \). Restrictions: \( x \neq -3 \) and \( x \neq 2 \).

2. You can only cancel factors, not terms. The +3 in the numerator is a TERM (added), not a factor (multiplied). \( x^2 + 3 \) does not factor further, so nothing cancels. The expression is already simplified.

2. Operations with Rational Expressions MCAP Essential

Operate on them just like numeric fractions, but with polynomials.

  • Multiply: Factor everything. Multiply straight across. Cancel common factors.
  • Divide: "Keep, Change, Flip." Multiply the first fraction by the reciprocal of the second. Then follow multiplication rules.
  • Add/Subtract: You MUST find a Least Common Denominator (LCD). Multiply the top and bottom of each fraction by the "missing" factors. Combine numerators over the single common denominator.
Example: Add \( \frac{2}{x+1} + \frac{x}{x-3} \)

LCD: \( (x+1)(x-3) \)

Rewrite each fraction: \( \frac{2(x-3)}{(x+1)(x-3)} + \frac{x(x+1)}{(x+1)(x-3)} \)

Expand tops: \( \frac{2x - 6}{\text{LCD}} + \frac{x^2 + x}{\text{LCD}} \)

Combine: \( \frac{x^2 + 3x - 6}{(x+1)(x-3)} \)

Check if the numerator factors further (it doesn't). Done.

Restrictions: \( x \neq -1, 3 \).

🎯 Practice — Operations

  1. (Multiply & Simplify) Simplify: \( \frac{x^2 - 4}{x + 1} \cdot \frac{x + 1}{x + 2} \)
  2. (Subtract) Simplify: \( \frac{3}{x - 2} - \frac{1}{x + 1} \)
Answers

1. \( \frac{(x-2)(x+2)}{x+1} \cdot \frac{x+1}{x+2} \). Cancel \( (x+1) \) and \( (x+2) \): Result = \( x - 2 \).

2. LCD = \( (x-2)(x+1) \). \( \frac{3(x+1) - 1(x-2)}{(x-2)(x+1)} = \frac{3x+3-x+2}{(x-2)(x+1)} = \frac{2x + 5}{(x-2)(x+1)} \).

3. Inverse Variation & The Parent Graph MCAP Focus

The simplest rational function is inverse variation: \( y = \frac{k}{x} \). As \( x \) gets larger, \( y \) gets smaller, and vice versa. \( k \) is the "constant of variation."

Real-World Analogy: 12 slices of pizza shared among \( x \) people. Each person gets \( y = \frac{12}{x} \) slices. 2 people = 6 slices each. 6 people = 2 slices each. 12 people = 1 slice each. As the number of people goes up, the slices per person goes down. That's inverse variation!
The Parent Function: \( f(x) = \frac{1}{x} \)
Its graph forms a hyperbola with two distinct branches in quadrants I and III. It has a vertical asymptote at \( x = 0 \) and horizontal asymptote at \( y = 0 \).
Example: Finding \( k \) from a table

If \( y \) varies inversely with \( x \), and \( y = 8 \) when \( x = 3 \), find the equation.

1. Start with the model: \( y = \frac{k}{x} \)

2. Plug in the known values: \( 8 = \frac{k}{3} \)

3. Solve for \( k \): \( k = 8 \times 3 = 24 \)

Equation: \( y = \frac{24}{x} \). Now you can find \( y \) for any \( x \).

🎯 Practice — Inverse Variation

  1. (Table → Equation) When \( x = 4 \), \( y = 6 \). If y varies inversely as x, find the equation and the value of y when \( x = 12 \).
  2. (Word Problem) The time to finish a job varies inversely with the number of workers. If 5 workers can finish in 8 hours, how long does it take 10 workers?
Answers

1. \( y = \frac{k}{x} \). \( 6 = \frac{k}{4} \implies k = 24 \). Equation: \( y = \frac{24}{x} \). When \( x = 12 \): \( y = 2 \).

2. \( t = \frac{k}{w} \). \( 8 = \frac{k}{5} \implies k = 40 \). With 10 workers: \( t = \frac{40}{10} = 4 \) hours.

4. Asymptotes & Holes MCAP Essential

Rational functions have distinct boundaries and breaks in their graphs.

Feature Definition How to Find It (For \( f(x) = \frac{N(x)}{D(x)} \))
Hole (Removable Discontinuity) A single missing point on the curve. Occurs when a factor cancels out completely from top and bottom. Set that canceled factor = 0 to find the x-coordinate.
Vertical Asymptote (VA) An invisible vertical line the graph approaches but never crosses. Set any remaining (uncanceled) factors in the denominator equal to 0.
Horizontal Asymptote (HA) An invisible horizontal line indicating the graph's end behavior. Compare Degrees of top (\( n \)) and bottom (\( m \)):
If \( n < m \): HA is \( y=0 \)
If \( n = m \): HA is \( y = \frac{a}{b} \) (ratio of leading coefficients)
If \( n > m \): No HA.

Why Does the Degree Rule for HA Work? (The Intuition)

Imagine plugging in \( x = 1{,}000{,}000 \). When \( x \) is astronomically large, the lower-order terms like \( +3x \) or \( -7 \) become irrelevant noise compared to the leading terms.

So \( \frac{5x^2 + 3x - 1}{2x^2 - 7} \) basically becomes \( \frac{5x^2}{2x^2} = \frac{5}{2} \). That's why HA = ratio of leading coefficients when degrees match!

And if the top degree is smaller? The top is "weaker" than the bottom, so the fraction crushes down to zero. Hence HA = \( y = 0 \).

Complete Example: Analyze \( f(x) = \frac{x^2 - 9}{x^2 - x - 6} \)

Step 1 — Factor everything:

Top: \( x^2 - 9 = (x-3)(x+3) \) (difference of squares)

Bottom: \( x^2 - x - 6 = (x-3)(x+2) \)

Step 2 — Hole? \( (x-3) \) cancels from top and bottom. Hole at \( x = 3 \).

To find the y-coordinate of the hole, plug \( x=3 \) into the simplified function: \( \frac{3+3}{3+2} = \frac{6}{5} \). Hole at \( (3, \frac{6}{5}) \).

Step 3 — VA? Remaining denominator factor: \( (x+2) = 0 \implies x = -2 \). VA at \( x = -2 \).

Step 4 — HA? Original degrees: top is 2, bottom is 2. Degrees match! HA = ratio of leading coefficients = \( \frac{1}{1} = 1 \). HA at \( y = 1 \).

Step 5 — x-intercept? Remaining numerator: \( (x+3) = 0 \implies x = -3 \). x-intercept at \( (-3, 0) \).

Tier 3 — Oblique (Slant) Asymptotes ★ Classroom Optional

If the degree of the numerator is EXACTLY ONE greater than the denominator (\( n = m + 1 \)), the graph has a diagonal "slant" asymptote instead of a horizontal one.

How to find it: Perform polynomial long division. The quotient (ignoring the remainder) is the equation of the line \( y = mx + b \) for the oblique asymptote.

5. Transformations of Rational Functions MCAP Essential

You can transform the parent function \( y = \frac{1}{x} \) just like parabolas. The general form is:

\( y = \frac{a}{x - h} + k \)
  • \( a \) (Vertical Stretch/Compression): Stretches branches further from the center. If negative, reflects across x-axis (branches move to Q2 and Q4).
  • \( h \) (Horizontal Shift): Moves the Vertical Asymptote Left/Right. \( (x - 3) \) moves VA to \( x=3 \).
  • \( k \) (Vertical Shift): Moves the Horizontal Asymptote Up/Down. \( + 4 \) moves HA to \( y=4 \).

🎯 Practice — Transformations

  1. (Interpretation) Describe ALL transformations applied to \( y = \frac{1}{x} \) to produce \( y = \frac{-3}{x + 2} + 1 \). State the asymptotes of the transformed function.
  2. (Reverse Engineering) Write the equation of a rational function in the form \( y = \frac{a}{x-h} + k \) with VA at \( x = -4 \), HA at \( y = 3 \), that passes through \( (-3, 5) \).
Answers

1. Reflected across x-axis (negative), vertically stretched by 3, shifted LEFT 2, shifted UP 1. VA: \( x = -2 \). HA: \( y = 1 \).

2. Start: \( y = \frac{a}{x - (-4)} + 3 = \frac{a}{x+4} + 3 \). Plug in \( (-3, 5) \): \( 5 = \frac{a}{-3+4} + 3 = a + 3 \implies a = 2 \). Equation: \( y = \frac{2}{x+4} + 3 \).

6. Graphing Rational Functions

Step-by-Step Master Procedure:

  1. Factor entirely! Numerator and denominator.
  2. Find Holes: Note any canceled factors.
  3. Find VA: Set remaining denominator factors to zero. Graph as dashed vertical lines.
  4. Find HA or SA: Compare highest degrees. Graph as dashed line.
  5. Find x-intercepts: Set remaining numerator factors to zero. Plot them.
  6. Find y-intercept: Plug in \( x = 0 \). Plot it.
  7. Sketch: Draw the branches hugging the asymptotes and passing through the intercepts. Use test points if a region is ambiguous.

🎯 Practice — Graphing Rational Functions

  1. (Multi-Part) Graph \( f(x) = \frac{2x}{x^2 - 1} \):
    a) Factor the denominator
    b) Find domain restrictions and vertical asymptotes
    c) Determine the horizontal asymptote
    d) Find x- and y-intercepts
    e) Describe the general shape
Answers

a) \( x^2 - 1 = (x-1)(x+1) \). No common factors → no holes.

b) \( x \neq 1 \) and \( x \neq -1 \). VA at \( x = 1 \) and \( x = -1 \).

c) Degree top (1) < degree bottom (2). HA: \( y = 0 \).

d) x-intercept: set numerator = 0 → \( 2x = 0 \implies x = 0 \). y-intercept: \( f(0) = 0 \). Both at origin.

e) Origin is both x- and y-intercept. Function approaches 0 far away (HA). Near \( x=1 \) and \( x=-1 \) it shoots to \( \pm \infty \). The function is odd (symmetric about origin), so left side mirrors right side flipped.

7. Solving Rational Equations MCAP Essential

The LCD Method

Equations have an equal sign. We can eliminate fractions entirely!

  1. Factor all denominators to find the Least Common Denominator (LCD).
  2. Note the domain constraints: \( x \) cannot equal any value that makes a denominator zero. These are extraneous solution traps!
  3. Multiply the ENTIRE equation (both sides, every single term) by the LCD. This will cancel every denominator.
  4. Solve the resulting polynomial equation (often linear or quadratic).
  5. Check your answers! Discard any answers that violate the domain constraints from step 2 (Extraneous Solutions).
Example: Solve \( \frac{3}{x-1} + \frac{1}{x} = \frac{7}{x(x-1)} \)

Step 1 (LCD): Denominators are \( (x-1) \), \( x \), and \( x(x-1) \). LCD = \( x(x-1) \).

Step 2 (Restrictions): \( x \neq 0 \) and \( x \neq 1 \).

Step 3 (Multiply everything by LCD):

\( x(x-1) \cdot \frac{3}{x-1} + x(x-1) \cdot \frac{1}{x} = x(x-1) \cdot \frac{7}{x(x-1)} \)

\( 3x + (x-1) = 7 \)

Step 4 (Solve): \( 3x + x - 1 = 7 \implies 4x = 8 \implies x = 2 \)

Step 5 (Check): Is \( x = 2 \) in our restriction list? No! So \( x = 2 \) is valid. ✓

🎯 Practice — Solving Rational Equations

  1. (Extraneous Trap) Solve \( \frac{x}{x-2} = \frac{2}{x-2} + 1 \). Check for extraneous solutions.
  2. (Multi-Step) Solve: \( \frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{x+2} = \frac{1}{3} \)
Answers

1. Multiply by \( (x-2) \): \( x = 2 + (x-2) = x \). This gives \( 0 = 0 \) — an identity! But \( x \neq 2 \) (restriction). So the solution is all real numbers except \( x = 2 \).

2. LCD = \( 3x(x+2) \). Multiply: \( 3(x+2) + 3x = x(x+2) \implies 6x + 6 = x^2 + 2x \implies x^2 - 4x - 6 = 0 \). Quadratic formula: \( x = 2 \pm \sqrt{10} \). Both valid.

8. Formative Check & Practice MCAP Essential

Practice:

  1. Simplify and state restrictions: \( \frac{x^2 - x - 12}{x^2 + 5x + 6} \)
  2. Perform the operation: \( \frac{3}{x+2} + \frac{5}{x-1} \)
  3. Find all asymptotes and holes for \( y = \frac{2x^2 - 8}{x^2 - 2x} \)
  4. Describe the transformations to the parent graph \( \frac{1}{x} \) for the function \( y = \frac{-1}{x+3} - 4 \).
  5. Solve for x: \( \frac{2}{x} + \frac{1}{2} = \frac{5}{x} \)
  6. (Error Analysis) A student simplifies \( \frac{x^2 + 5x}{x} \) as \( x^2 + 5 \) by "canceling the x's." Is this correct? If not, show the correct simplification.
  7. (Inverse Variation) The intensity of light varies inversely with the square of the distance from the source. At 2 meters, intensity is 100 units. Find the intensity at 5 meters.
  8. (Extraneous) Solve \( \frac{x+1}{x-1} = \frac{2}{x-1} \). Does the equation have a valid solution?
Solutions (Expand to check)

1. Simplify: Top is \( (x-4)(x+3) \), bottom is \( (x+2)(x+3) \). Cancel \( (x+3) \). Result: \( \frac{x-4}{x+2} \). Restrictions: \( x \neq -2, -3 \).

2. Addition: LCD is \( (x+2)(x-1) \). Top becomes \( 3(x-1) + 5(x+2) = 8x + 7 \). Result: \( \frac{8x+7}{(x+2)(x-1)} \).

3. Features: Factor top \( 2(x-2)(x+2) \). Factor bottom \( x(x-2) \). The \( (x-2) \) cancels → hole at \( x=2 \) (y-coord: 4). VA at \( x=0 \). HA: \( y = 2 \).

4. Transformations: Reflected across x-axis, shifted Left 3, shifted Down 4. VA: \( x=-3 \), HA: \( y=-4 \).

5. Solve: LCD = \( 2x \). \( 4 + x = 10 \implies x = 6 \). Valid ✓.

6. WRONG! You can only cancel factors. Since \( x \) is a factor of the entire numerator: \( \frac{x(x+5)}{x} = x + 5 \). Student cancelled inside a term, not a factor.

7. \( I = \frac{k}{d^2} \). \( 100 = \frac{k}{4} \implies k = 400 \). At 5m: \( I = \frac{400}{25} = 16 \) units.

8. Multiply by \( (x-1) \): \( x+1 = 2 \implies x = 1 \). But \( x = 1 \) makes the denominator zero! This is extraneous. The equation has NO solution.
