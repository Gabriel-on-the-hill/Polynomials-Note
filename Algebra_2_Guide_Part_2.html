Foundation F4 — Operations (The Box)

Tier 1 — Quick Reference

Stop FOILing. Start Boxing.

  1. Draw a grid (2×2 for binomial×binomial; expand to 2×3 for degree 2 × degree 1).
  2. Write the terms on the outside. Keep negative signs attached to the term.
  3. Multiply term-by-term into each cell.
  4. Combine like terms and write in standard form (highest degree first).

Tip: Use the box method for larger products to prevent missing terms or sign errors.

Tier 2 — Deep Mastery

Conservation of Degree: Multiplying a degree-2 polynomial by a degree-1 polynomial yields degree 3 unless coefficients cancel.

Standard Form: Arrange polynomial terms from highest to lowest power; constant term last.

Tier 3 — Tutor Notes

Force the box method for anything larger than binomial×binomial. If a sign is missed inside the box, the final polynomial will be wrong — check boxes before combining.

Foundation F5 — The Factoring Factory

Tier 1 — Quick Reference (Flowchart)

  1. GCF first. Always check for a common factor.
  2. Count terms:
    • 2 terms — Difference of squares? (a² − b² = (a−b)(a+b)).
    • 3 terms — Trinomial: find two numbers that add to the middle term and multiply to the constant (or use slide/divide if a > 1).
    • 4 terms — Try grouping.

Tier 2 — Deep Mastery

Factoring is the inverse of multiplication — think of it as division. Roots, zeros, x-intercepts and solutions are interchangeable terms: they all identify the x-values where the polynomial evaluates to zero.

Tier 3 — Tutor Notes

If the student pauses, ask: “How many terms?” That triggers the decision flow. Remind them to factor completely — e.g., (x² − 4) → (x − 2)(x + 2).

Foundation F6 — Graphing & Behavior

Tier 1 — Quick Reference

  • End behavior: Determined by leading coefficient & degree.
    • Odd degree: ends in opposite directions.
    • Even degree: ends in same direction.
  • Multiplicity of zeros:
    • Multiplicity 1 — graph crosses (cut)
    • Multiplicity 2 — graph touches and turns (bounce)
    • Multiplicity 3 — flatten & cross (jog)

Tier 2 — Deep Mastery

The Zero Product Property: if a product equals zero, at least one factor must be zero — use this to identify x-values that make the polynomial zero.

Tier 3 — Tutor Notes

For this course, focus on shape & key intercepts. Save precise turning-point computation for calculus. Encourage sketching before plotting.
